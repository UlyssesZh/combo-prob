\title{Project description}

\begin{document}

Consider this problem (sometimes called the longest run statistics \cite{makri})
about a random bit string:
in a sequence of length $N$ of independent events with the same probability distribution
on a finite number of possible outcomes,
what is the probability distribution of the length of the longest substring such that
all events in the substring are of a specific outcome?
The broken stick problem is:
given a stick of length $1$,
if we break it at $k$ random points, it will be broken into $k+1$ pieces,
and what is the probability distribution of the length of the longest piece?
It turns out that the two problems are related:
the continuum limit of the former problem is the generating function of the answer to the latter problem.

The formal statement of the problem is as follows:
consider a random bit string of length $N\in\bN$, where each bit is independent and
has probability $p\in\b{0,1}$ of being $1$.
Let $\fc{P_{N,S}}p$ be the probability that the length of the longest all-$1$ substring
of the bit string is $S\in\bN$.
What is the expression of $\fc{P_{N,S}}p$?
The recurrence relation is
\[P_{N,0<S<N}=\p{1-p}\p{p^S\sum_{s=0}^SP_{N-S-1,s}+\sum_{s=0}^{S-1}P_{N-s-1,S}},\]
with initial conditions
\[P_{N,0}=\p{1-p}^N,\quad P_{N,N}=p^N,\quad P_{N,S>N}=0.\]
Then, $P_{N,S}$ is a polynomial of degree $N$ in $p$.

The continuum limit is defined as
\[\fc f{P,x}\ceq\lim_{N\to\infty}N\fc{P_{N,xN}}{P^{1/N}},\quad P\in\b{0,1},\quad x\ge0,\]
which is a probability distribution of $x\ceq S/N$ with $P\ceq p^N$ as the parameter.
Its exact expression is
\[\fc f{P=0,x}=\fc\dlt x,\quad\fc f{P\in\left(0,1\right],x}=\begin{dcases}
	0,&x=0,\\\text{complicated},&x\in\p{0,1},\\P\fc\dlt{x-1},&x\ge1.
\end{dcases}\]
where
\begin{align*}
	\text{complicated}&=-P^x\ln P\p{2+\ln P^{x-1}}
	+\ln P\sum_{k=2}^{\ceil{1/x}-1}\fr{k\p{-1}^k}{\p{k-2}!}P^{kx}\p{\ln P^{kx-1}}^{k-2}\\
	&\phantom{=}{}\times\p{1+\fr2{k-1}\ln P^{kx-1}+\fr1{k\p{k-1}}\p{\ln P^{kx-1}}^2}.
\end{align*}

On the other hand, the continuum limit is also the probability distribution of the longest interval
without occurrences in the Poisson process on the interval $\b{0,1}$ with rate $-\ln P$.
When there are $k$ occurrences each at positions $x_1<\cdots<x_k$, it divides the interval into
$k+1$ intervals:
\[S_0=x_1,\quad S_1=x_2-x_1,\quad\ldots,\quad S_k=1-x_k.\]
Order them into $S_{\p0}\le\cdots\le S_{\p k}$.
The probability distribution of the longest interval is then $\fc f{x,P}$.

If the number $k$ is not Poisson distributed but fixed instead,
the problem is called the broken stick problem, and the probability distribution of $S_{\p k}$ is \cite{holst}
\[\fc{f_{k>0}}x=\sum_{j=0}^{k+1}-jk\binom{k+1}j\p{-1}^j\opc{max}{1-jx,0}^{k-1},\quad
\fc{f_0}x=\fc\dlt{x-1}.\]
The answers to the two problems are related:
\[\fc f{x,P}=\sum_{k=0}^\infty\fr{P\p{-\ln P}^k}{k!}\fc{f_k}x.\]
Therefore, we can see that $\fc f{x,P}/P$ as a function of $-\ln P$
is the exponential generating function of $\fc{f_k}x$.
